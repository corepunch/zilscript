# Horror.zil Adventure Walkthrough

## Overview

This is a complete walkthrough for the horror.zil adventure game - an expansion of a spooky, eerie abandoned sanitarium adventure with 22 rooms and 57 objects.

**Setting:** Blackwood Sanitarium (Est. 1898, Closed 1952)  
**Genre:** Medical horror with Gothic atmosphere  
**Style:** Zork-like technical precision with environmental storytelling

## Puzzle Elements

1. **Key Puzzles:** Multiple keys to find and use
   - Brass key → drawer
   - Safe key → safe
   - Chapel key → chapel

2. **Steam Valve Puzzle:** Turn valve to create steam that opens corroded door to Hydrotherapy Room

3. **Light Source:** Working flashlight at the start, oil lantern available later (old flashlight in boiler room is a red herring with dead batteries)

4. **Chain Cutting:** Use scalpel to cut through chains blocking the Morgue

5. **Safe Puzzle:** Find safe key in desk drawer to unlock wall safe containing chapel key

6. **Story Clues:** Progressive revelation through documents:
   - Patient Ledger mentions Patient 237
   - Dr. Mordecai's journal reveals experiments
   - Wall scratches point to Patient 189 in chapel
   - Medical records show patient resistance to treatment

## Story Overview

The adventure unfolds the dark history of Blackwood Sanitarium through environmental storytelling:

- **The Setting:** A Victorian-era abandoned sanitarium (1898-1952) with Gothic atmosphere
- **The Mystery:** Two interconnected patient stories (Patient 237 and Patient 189)
- **The Horror:** Medical experiments that went beyond the boundaries of science and morality
- **The Resolution:** Freeing Patient 189 from decades of torment, providing narrative closure

### Key Story Elements

- **Dr. Heinrich Mordecai:** The sanitarium's director who conducted terrible experiments
- **Patient 237:** Subject of Compound 237 experiments
- **Patient 189:** The ultimate result of Dr. Mordecai's work - someone who "transcended death itself"
- **The Chapel:** Final location where Patient 189 has been waiting since the sanitarium's closure
